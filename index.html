🛡 Security Tips Dashboard
🔐 Use a unique password for every account. Reusing passwords is the #1 cause of credential breaches.
📏 Longer is stronger. A 20-character passphrase beats a complex 8-character password in both security and memorability.
🎲 Always use a cryptographically secure random generator (like this app). Human-chosen passwords are predictable.
🔄 Rotate passwords for critical accounts every 90 days, or immediately if a service you use reports a data breach.
🛡 Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) wherever possible — it stops over 99% of automated attacks even if your password is leaked.
💾 Store passwords in a reputable password manager (Bitwarden, KeePass, 1Password) — never in plaintext files or spreadsheets.
🚫 Never share passwords over SMS, email, or chat apps. These channels are not encrypted end-to-end at the server level.
👀 Beware of phishing. Always check the URL before entering credentials. Bookmark critical sites like banking portals.
📵 Don't log in to sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi without a trusted VPN.
🧠 A passphrase like "River-Castle-Moon-92" has higher entropy than "P@ss1!" and is far easier to remember.
